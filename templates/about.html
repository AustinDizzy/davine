About

Welcome to Davine, a social analytics service built for the popular social media network Vine (heads up, we're in no way affiliated with or endorsed by Vine Labs, Inc).

We track and calculate various statistics about every Vine user we discover, and we show you all that data in pretty charts and graphs. If you'd like, we can even give you those pretty charts and graphs in a free weekly email report. For content creators - especially those who have a prominent presence on Vine - these are important to optimizing your content so you reach the bulk of your target audience.

Davine is also open data, allowing any and all users to download any and all data Davine has ever collected or created. We're also open source, ad-free, and no vines or amazonian flora were harmed during the making of Davine.

We are working on making Davine better as our dataset grows. As we collect data, we'll be inventing new ways to browse the data and correlate interesting trends.

F.A.Q.

What is this?
Davine is a social analytics service built for Vine. In English, we can track how much and where your profile is growing by and show it all to you in a few pretty graphs and tables. In tech speak, we're an Open Data service that crawls Vine for users to track daily metrics for. We're fully open source, open data, and ad-free.
How are profiles added to Davine?
Davine tries to discover users all on its own by monitoring the feeds of all Vine channels and playlists. If we can't find your profile or a profile you're looking for, you can submit the profile manually by submitting the direct link to the Vine profile on our homepage. For example, if I wanted to submit SpaceX's profile, I would submit https://vine.co/SpaceX. If you are unsure what the direct link to your profile is, it would be best to login to https://vine.co to find your URL.
If you are still having trouble, you can submit your profile to Davine by opening the Vine app, going to your profile tab, tap "Settings", select "Invite via email", and send the invitation to "share@davine.co". You should receive an email response if your submission was successful.
Who is this?
I'll tell you one legal entity we aren't, Vine. We are in no way affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Vine Labs, Inc or Twitter Inc. We're just one college kid and a noble investor, both trying to make something useful out of our time and skills.
I do not want Davine to track my profile's metrics. How can I prevent Davine from doing so?
In order to prevent Davine, and any other third-party service, from scraping your publicly available Vine information, your profile and posts must be set to Private. Read the Vine FAQ page to learn how to do this.
You say you're open source and open data. Where can I access the source and all data?
The Davine project is FOSS licensed under the MIT Open Source License. You can access the source, and report any issues, on its Github page. You can download the data for an individual user on their profile page here on Davine. Simply click the " Download" button when on a user's page.
If you are looking for direct access to the entire Vine dataset, please sign up to be notified when we launch access.
How does the Featured Profile work?
Once a week, Davine picks a random user that it discovered that week or is receiving email reports and displays that person's profile and latest Vine on our homepage. Think of this as free publicity. If you are interested in purchasing priority as a featured profile for a week, please contact us
